1

Tiak-Tiak

Pioneer Product Lead

Creating a brand-new product can pose significant challenges, but when approached with the right strategy, it transforms into an exciting journey. Allow me to guide you through my experience of orchestrating the entire lifecycle of this remarkable product, starting from its inception and culminating in the successful launch of the Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

1. Problem Identitification: Identified consumer pain point.

2. Assumption Approach: Documented all assumptions related to the identified problem..

3. Market Research: Collaborated with the team to conduct market research to Validate or Invalidate some assumptions. NB: Research questions weren't merely hypothetical; we conducted research that engaged potential customers in conversations, allowing them to articulate their pain points. This approach not only led to the identification of solvable problems but also fostered a deeper understanding of their needs.

4. Empathy Map: Immersed myself in the user's perspective, experiencing both the PAIN they currently endure and the GAIN they stand to achieve with our product.

5. Systems Thinking Approach: Deconstructing the complexity of the product's challenges to gain a comprehensive understanding of various approaches and their potential impact on the product's outcome.

6. User Persona: Through meticulous analysis, I developed well-defined user personas, which fortified our case and guided our problem-solving approach based on user segmentation.

7. Fear and Hopes: Outlined the Fears and Hopes regarding the product so I could better tackle research in what could.MAKE or BREAK the product

8. Business Model Canvas (BMC): Articulated a comprehensive business model to discern and engage all stakeholders involved.

9. Defined the customer journey using a high-fidelity prototype (HFP) for a more detailed and clear representation.

10. Value Proposition: Crafted a well-structured and transparent value proposition that caters to the needs of both users and riders.

11. Product Prototype: This invaluable tool allowed us to assess user interaction with the product, avoiding unnecessary expenditure of time and resources on a solution that might not fully engage the market.

11a. Prototype Iteration: Implemented revisions informed by customer feedback gathered during A/B testing.

12. Product Development: Teamed up with software engineers to jointly develop and rigorously test the product.

13. Go To Market (GTM): Formulated a well-defined Go To Market strategy (GTM) to launch the product effectively.

14. Competitor Analysis: Carefully identified competitors and assessed their strengths, enabling us to tailor our solution to fill the gaps in the market and secure market share while remaining competitive.

15. Product Marketing: Worked in close collaboration with the marketing and sales teams to develop compelling marketing content aimed at driving product sales

16. Product Roadmap: Articulated a well-defined product roadmap outlining our strategic direction and key initiatives for the upcoming three years

17. Copywriting: Executed content localization by crafting tailored and culturally relevant copy.

18.Achievements: Attained a user base of 2,000 engaged and paying customers within 12 months. Successfully managed the team, meeting set goals within the allocated budget and timeframe while prioritizing tasks effectively.

FrameWorks Used:

SWOT Analysis, PESTAL Analysis, Hopes and Fears, System Thinking, Design Thinking

Software Management Tool:

Jira, Confluence, Slack, Zoom, Google meet, Figma, Canva
